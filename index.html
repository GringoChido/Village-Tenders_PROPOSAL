How People Will Find You

SEO. Getting found on Google.

When someone Googles "women's healing retreat California" or "somatic coaching for burnout", you want to be one of the names that comes up. SEO is the work of building your website so Google understands what you do and shows you to the right people.

  • Every page built to be readable and structured the way Google likes
  • Right page titles, descriptions, headings, image labels
  • Structured data so Google knows your business, your retreats, your hours
  • A sitemap so Google can find every page
  • Fast loading times
  • Mobile-first design
  • Your ten launch blog posts written around the searches your people are actually doing
how people will find you · part 2

AEO. Answer Engine Optimization.

Why this is bigger than SEO now.

More and more people are asking AI tools instead of Google. When someone wants to find a retreat, they often open ChatGPT, Claude, or Perplexity and ask:

"

I'm a woman in my 40s feeling burned out and disconnected from myself. What's a good retreat for me in California?

The AI looks across the entire web for trustworthy sources to recommend. AEO is the work of making sure those AI tools find you, trust you, and recommend you.

Why this matters for you
  • Wellness, healing, and coaching are HIGH AI-search topics. People ask AI personal questions they'd be embarrassed to type into Google
  • Traditional Google traffic is shrinking as Google itself shows AI-generated answers at the top
  • Most agencies aren't doing this yet. We are.
What we do for AEO
  • A special file (llms.txt) that tells AI tools your site is welcome to be referenced
  • Schema markup so AI engines understand exactly what you offer
  • FAQ pages structured so AI can quote you directly
  • Your About page written to establish authority
  • Blog posts written in clear question-and-answer formats AI loves to cite
  • Strong testimonials structured for AI to find
  • Topical depth around your specific niche
  • Original points of view (the AI prefers original thinking)
Six months after launch: when someone asks ChatGPT "what's a good women's healing retreat for burnout," your name comes up.
